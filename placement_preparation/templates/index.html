Interview Tips

Expert advice to ace your placement interviews

Common HR Questions

Tell me about yourself. +
Start with your educational background, mention 1-2 key projects or achievements, state your technical skills, and end with why you're excited about this role. Keep it under 2 minutes. Practice until it sounds natural, not rehearsed.
Why do you want to join our company? +
Research the company beforehand! Mention specific things: their products, mission, culture, growth, or a recent achievement. Connect it to your own goals. Never say "for money" or "for job security."
What are your strengths and weaknesses? +
Strengths: Pick 2-3 relevant to the role with examples. Weaknesses: Choose a real but not critical flaw, and always show what you're doing to improve it. Example: "I sometimes overthink, so I've started using the 80/20 rule to decide faster."
Where do you see yourself in 5 years? +
Show ambition aligned with the company's growth. "I see myself growing into a senior technical role, contributing to significant product decisions, and mentoring junior developers." Don't mention competitor companies or unrelated fields.
Why should we hire you? +
This is your sales pitch! Highlight 3 things: your relevant technical skills, a proven achievement (with numbers if possible), and your eagerness to contribute to this specific team. Be confident but not arrogant.
